THE ROAD NOT TAKEN

Poem by Robert Frost

This well-known poem is about making choices, and the choices that shape us.
Robert Frost is an American poet who writes simply, but insightfully,about common,ordinary experiences.

Two roads diverged in a yellow wood,
And sorry I could not travel both
 And be one traveler, long I stood
And looked down one as far as I could
To where it bent in the undergrowth;

       
      Then took the other, as just as fair,
And having perhaps the better claim,
Though as for that the passing there
Had worn them really about the same,


      And both that morning equally lay
In leaves no step had trodden black.
Oh, I kept the first for another day!>

Main theme of the poem :

The main theme of the poem, "The Road Not Taken," is that human beings are confronted with and defined by the choices they make.The speaker can only choose one path, and must abide by that choice. The theme of the poem is that human beings are defined by the choices they face and the choices they make.
